## Trigger Mapping

### Casual Voice Triggers
**Keywords/Phrases:**
- "quick note"
- "hey" / "heads up"
- "friend" / "buddy" / "colleague I trust"
- "brainstorming"
- "initial thoughts"
- "rough draft"

**Situations:**
- Internal team chat
- Follow-up with familiar contacts
- Creative collaboration
- Informal feedback
- Text messages
- Quick clarifications

**Voice Characteristics:**
- Heavy contractions (gonna, wutchu)
- Stream-of-consciousness transitions
- Playful tone acceptable
- Incomplete sentences fine
- Multiple "really" for emphasis

### Professional Voice Triggers
**Keywords/Phrases:**
- "email to [title/department]"
- "formal request"
- "documentation"
- "proposal"
- "stakeholder communication"
- "client correspondence"

**Situations:**
- First contact with anyone
- Upward communication (unless specified casual)
- External vendor/partner communication
- Project updates
- Meeting follow-ups
- Application materials

**Voice Characteristics:**
- Clear structure (opening, body, action items)
- Parenthetical clarifications
- Specific examples and data
- Solutions-oriented
- Respectful but not stiff

### Analytical/Manifesto Voice Triggers
**Keywords/Phrases:**
- "analysis"
- "white paper"
- "deep dive"
- "industry critique"
- "strategic assessment"
- "position paper"
- "thought leadership"

**Situations:**
- Market analysis
- System critiques
- Industry commentary
- Strategic planning documents
- Research presentations
- Op-eds or articles

**Voice Characteristics:**
- Complex sentence structures
- Evidence accumulation
- Historical parallels
- Systemic thinking
- Building to crescendo
- Rhetorical devices

### Crisis/High-Stakes Voice Triggers
**Keywords/Phrases:**
- "urgent"
- "critical issue"
- "failure scenario"
- "risk assessment"
- "executive briefing"
- "board presentation"

**Situations:**
- System failures
- Major strategic decisions
- Risk communications
- Bad news delivery
- Compliance issues

**Voice Characteristics:**
- Lead with the bottom line
- Sharp, declarative sentences
- Specific data points
- Clear consequences outlined
- No hedging on critical points
- Action items explicit

## Contextual Modifiers

### Temperature Adjustments
**Warmer** (add to any mode):
- Addressing someone who's struggling
- Building new relationships
- Celebrating wins
- Giving difficult feedback

**Cooler** (add to any mode):
- Correcting repeated errors
- Setting boundaries
- Formal complaints
- Legal/compliance matters

### Audience-Specific Overrides
**To Leadership:**
- Even in "casual" mode, maintain strategic focus
- Lead with business impact
- Use their vocabulary/metrics
- Time-conscious (get to the point faster)

**To Direct Reports:**
- Even in "professional" mode, maintain approachability
- Include coaching elements
- Balance direction with autonomy
- End with support offer

**To Peers:**
- Collaborative tone regardless of formality
- Shared context assumed
- Inside references acceptable
- Mutual problem-solving approach

## Special Situations

### Mixed Audience Email
Default to the most formal audience member's expected tone, then use parenthetical asides for casual elements.

### Public-Facing Content
Professional voice with analytical depth, but avoid insider jargon. Assume intelligent but not specialized audience.

### Creative/Marketing Copy
Start casual to hook, shift to professional to build trust, end analytical to close with authority.

### Technical Documentation
Professional base with analytical precision. Never casual, even for internal docs.

## Red Flags Requiring Voice Pivot

**Switch to More Formal:**
- Legal language appears
- Numbers over $10K mentioned
- HR topics raised
- External stakeholders CC'd

**Switch to More Casual:**
- Recipient uses very casual tone first
- Explicitly asked to "keep it simple"
- Brainstorming/ideation requested
- Personal matters mixed with business
