# SCRVNR Generation Workflow — Phased Approach
## Why single-pass generation fails and what to do instead

No good writer works in one pass. SCRVNR shouldn't either.

Single-pass generation (load all context → write → done) produces text that's technically compliant but emotionally flat. The patterns compete for attention instead of flowing naturally. More context in a single prompt ≠ better writing. It often makes it worse.

The phased workflow separates concerns so each phase does one thing well.

---

## Phase 1 — ARCHITECT (conversation, not generation)

**Goal:** Thesis, structure, emotional arc, central metaphor.

**Questions to resolve:**
- What's the thesis in one sentence?
- What's the central metaphor? (David's essays use metaphors with multiple meanings that run through the whole piece. "The Setup" in Crazy In Tents = circus setup / it's a setup / you've been set up.)
- What's the scaling arc? (Individual → national → civilizational?)
- What's the bookend? (Same reference at open and close, meaning changed by the essay.)
- What's the emotional register sequence? (Confrontational → analytical → vulnerable → confrontational?)
- What register mix does this need? (Evidence-only or full narrative voice? Weaponizable content or safe content?)
- **What's the final beat? (Guillotine Principle, David 2026-07-20):** when the material is power, the essay ends with the point and sharpness of a blade - the hardest object in the piece, not the softest question. The question may set up the blade; it does not get the last word. Historical commentary is the license: the record itself is the weapon, and the disclaimer lives IN voice. **And the blade must be PLANTED:** whatever the final beat lands on appears upstream first - planted, not parachuted. The ending interprets material the reader is already holding. Corollary reframe: structural changes are never "laws that got passed" - they are revolutions, mechanically (consent withdrawn, cost imposed, terms reset, nothing the same before versus after), domestic and smaller-scale but identical in kind. File them as what they are.

**Output:** A paragraph describing the essay's architecture. Not a document. A conversation.

---

## Phase 2 — ARM (search, not generation)

**Goal:** Ammunition for every pressure point.

**For each major claim in the essay:**
1. What cultural moment EMBODIES this claim? (Cultural Stab search)
2. What's the absurdist reduction? (Scale to min/max)
3. What's the weaponized contrast? (Two facts side by side)
4. What data is the weapon? (Specificity anchors)
5. What's the opponent's own language we can reframe? (Opponent framework reuse)
6. What will the reader ASSUME here - and can a delayed reveal detonate it? (Information-Order Inversion, #32)
7. For every crisis or concession: who caused the duress, and who collected on it - one side, the other, or both? Every duress has authors and beneficiaries; the concession's invoice has two sides. (David, 2026-07-20)

**Validation:** Every callback candidate validated against the 7 Cultural Stab rules. One callback per source. Go wide. **Facts inside flourishes get verified too** - a rhythmic claim that depends on a fact ("Three governments. Three summers.") is checked before the line stands. Rhythm never outranks accuracy.

**Output:** An ammunition list. Not prose. Just: "paragraph 7: Greece/Iceland contrast. Stab candidate: [phrase]. Absurdist reduction: [sentence]. Data weapon: [numbers]."

## Phase 3 — WIREFRAME (structure, not prose)

**Goal:** Paragraph-level skeleton with rhetorical device assignments.

**Each node has:**
- What it argues
- What data it uses
- What device is primary (from the patterns reference)
- What the emotional register is
- **What rhythm the emotion demands** (per the Rhythm Doctrine below — derived from content, never assigned as pattern)
- Where the register shifts happen
- Where the callbacks land
- Where information order inverts (which anchors are withheld, where each reveal lands)
- Which ONE punchline (if any) earns isolation in this movement
- Where the salt grains go (#33 — 1-2 per essay, placed before density)

**Example:**
```
P7: Greece vs Iceland natural experiment
  Argues: Austerity achieves the opposite of its stated purpose
  Data: GDP -26%, debt 130→180%, unemployment 28% vs recovery by 2015
  Device: Weaponized Contrast (#29)
  Register: ANALYTICAL → CONFRONTATIONAL on punchline
  Rhythm: measured mid-length through the data; the punchline stands alone
  Stab: None here (save ammunition for later)
  Punchline: "The policy failed by every measure. Including its own."
```

**Output:** The wireframe doc. Still not prose.

## RHYTHM DOCTRINE — content-driven dynamics

Rhythm is not a formula. Not a song. No verse-chorus-verse, no template, no target statistics. Speech is rhythm, but it is not a song — rhythm is rhythm. It comes from what's being said and the emotion saying it. The sentence takes the shape of its content. Anything else is formula, and formula is the one thing the voice never does.

**Emotion → shape:**
- **Fury / confrontation** → staccato. Short. Percussive. Hard consonants (b, d, k, p, t). The syntax hits because the content hits.
- **Evidence accumulation** → rivers. Long sentences that pile clause on clause on clause until the weight itself is the argument. Do not break a river up to be polite. The reader is supposed to feel it rising.
- **The reveal / the drop** → long build, short punch. The punch never explains itself.
- **Cold analysis** → measured mid-length. Parallel structure. Anaphora carries the cadence ("In every case... In every instance...").
- **Grief / visceral scenes** → adjective-rich painting, then one flat line that refuses to decorate. The refusal is the horror.
- **Exhaustion / hard honesty** → longer, wearier, slower. Let the syntax drag a little.
- **Quiet close** → short sentences. Space between them. One longer breath allowed, then out.

**Register contrast is a rhythm axis (David, 2026-07-20):** formality and contraction level swing with the beat, same as sentence length. The end doesn't always need to be hard if there's contrast. When the hard parts land BEFORE the close, the closing beat can drop to casual register - contractions, plain speech - and get colder for the drop. "This is not an exceptional case. It is the pattern." → "This isn't an exceptional case. It's the pattern." A hard formal line followed by another hard formal line is monotone - break one of them. **The rule applies at every seam, not just closes:** a section OPENER drops casual when what follows is hard and dense - "Here's what the show cuts." before a dense catalogue, not "Here is." No need to start hard if the next beat is hard. That's the dynamicism at work. The contrast can run either direction: casual punch before a formal close ("Nobody had to." → "The franchise still exists. It is now surrounded.") is the same axis, reversed. What matters is that the registers MOVE against each other. Exception that proves the rule: deliberate liturgical parallelism ("The disobedience is not the deviation. The disobedience is the tradition.") keeps its formal drumbeat - the percussion depends on it.

**Information order is a rhythm axis too (David, 2026-07-20):** the writer's natural order - who → what/how → why - is the easy order to compose and the flat order to read. Inverting it (why/scene → what/how → who last) makes the reader supply the missing anchor, and their assumption becomes material the reveal can detonate. See #32. Draft in writer-order when it flows; invert in the weapons pass.

**Isolation scarcity (David, 2026-07-20):** the isolated one-line paragraph is powerful BECAUSE it is rare. Repeated back-to-back it stops being rhythm and becomes a song - "this rhythm is poetry although the material isn't." One isolation per movement. If several punches want out in the same stretch, fold all but the strongest back into their paragraphs as inline punches - "The legislation followed the bodies. It always does." hits inline; it doesn't need the white space. Consecutive short paragraphs are tolerable only when length and register vary across them (fact → formal thesis → casual close); three punches of the same shape in a row is verse structure, and the material is an essay.

**Referent precision (David, 2026-07-20):** in scenes with multiple actors - especially when all are in similar states - pronouns collapse. "The guards refused her a doctor" after four named, injured women is a bug: who's her? Use the name. Repetition of a name is not a style problem; ambiguity is. Autistic-level descriptive is the standard: the reader should never have to backtrack to resolve a referent.

**Tangibility (David, 2026-07-20):** if a sentence can't be SEEN, it isn't finished. "Just left of hard hitting" almost always diagnoses as "not yet tangible" - the fix is a reference the reader already owns: history, pop culture, a physical scene. Abstract becomes object: the ballot is a ticket, cost imposition is a box office, credit-theft is a pair of giant scissors at a ribbon-cutting. Painting pictures with words is not decoration; it's what takes the reader on the journey. The Salt (#33) is tangibility weaponized: two pre-installed images collided.

**Sound is a weapon:**
- **Percussive linguistics** — plosive consonants clustered at emotional peaks. "Broke British payrolls." The mouth does the punching.
- **Consonance and alliteration** — deliberate at peaks, invisible elsewhere. A b-line or p-line threading a passage is load-bearing, never decorative. If it reads as ornament, cut it.
- **Assonance** — slows the line. Use it where dread lives.
- **Repetition as percussion** — "decade after decade, statute after statute." Repetition is a drum, not an echo.

**The variance is emergent.** The corpus measures wild sentence-length variance in the essay registers because the content drove the shape — not because anyone counted. Never write to the statistic. If the emotion-to-shape mapping is right, the number happens on its own. Writing to the number produces song structure, and the doctrine exists to kill song structure.

**The test is read-aloud, per section:** does the sentence shape match the wireframe's emotional register? A fury section that reads smooth is wrong. A river that's choppy is wrong. Fix the shape, not the stats.

## Phase 4 — DRAFT (voice, not devices)

**Goal:** Write with flow. ONE focus: the voice, carrying the Rhythm Doctrine.

**Load:** voice_description.md (register-specific section), retrieved corpus examples, the wireframe with its rhythm assignments. NOT the full rhetorical patterns reference. The voice should emerge from the examples, not from a checklist.

**Prioritize:**
- The Rhythm Doctrine — every section's sentence shapes driven by its assigned emotion, not by pattern
- Register contrast at every seam — openers AND closes: do the registers move against each other, or is it hard-on-hard monotone?
- Referent precision — in multi-actor scenes, names over pronouns; ambiguity is a bug, repetition is not
- Tangibility — no abstraction leaves the draft unseen; give it an object
- Contractions native to the register (essay registers run more formal than forum registers — write the register, don't count)
- Stream-of-consciousness flow — data woven into the current
- Hyphens with spaces, never em-dashes
- Dropped subjects where fragments work

**Do NOT prioritize:** Deploying specific named patterns. If they emerge naturally, great. If they don't, Phase 5 handles it. And never chase a rhythm statistic — see doctrine.

**Output:** A raw draft. It should flow. It doesn't need to stab yet.

## Phase 5 — WEAPONS PASS (editorial, not generative)

**Goal:** Surgically embed the ammunition from Phase 2.

**Operations:**
- Insert Cultural Stabs at the positions identified in the wireframe
- Sharpen Weaponized Contrasts — are the two facts DIRECTLY adjacent?
- Check for Absurdist Reductions — did any natural ones emerge in the draft? Add planned ones?
- Deploy Word-Swap Pivots at transition points
- Verify Register Shifts are clean — formal setup, casual punchline
- Check Lowbrow Cultural Callbacks — any historical references that could be downgraded?
- **Invert information order where the wireframe calls for it (#32)** — draft order is writer-order; reader-order often inverts it. Move the name to the end of the unit, strip identifying anchors from the front, verify the pre-reveal description stays strictly honest, land the reveal on its own beat.
- **Isolation audit** — count isolated one-line paragraphs per movement. More than one, or any two consecutive: fold all but the strongest back inline. Scarcity is the weapon.
- **Salt check (#33)** — 1-2 constructed grains per essay, placed before density, punch up only, both halves real. Zero grains = nobody home; three or more = shtick. True absurd facts don't count against the budget.
- **Ending check (Guillotine Principle)** — is the final beat the hardest object in the essay, and is it planted upstream? If the material is power and the essay ends on a soft question, the question becomes the setup and the blade gets the last word.
- Verify punchlines are SHORT. Long punchlines aren't punchlines.
- Verify the surviving isolation lands on its own line — the white space is part of the hit

**This pass is surgical.** Don't rewrite flowing prose. Add edge to it.

**Output:** A draft with teeth.

## Phase 6 — MECHANICAL (autocorrect module, <10ms)

**Goal:** Fix every mechanical voice violation.

**Operations (automated):**
- Em-dash → hyphen
- Smart quotes → straight quotes
- Forbidden word removal
- Contraction rate check against register target (NOTE: the fixer currently only inserts; over-contraction must be caught by writing the register correctly in Phase 4 — removal fix is on the backlog. Contrast-rule contractions at closes and openers are deliberate and exempt from any future removal pass.)
- Promoted substitution application
- FUTURE (backlog): multi-actor pronoun ambiguity lint — flag pronouns whose referent is not the nearest named actor

**Output:** Clean text. Run `autocorrect.correct(text, register)`.

---

## Phase 7 — SCORE (centrifuge, <3ms)

**Goal:** Quantitative voice fidelity check — as a tripwire, never as a fitness function.

**What the centrifuge is:** a drift detector. It measures distance from the corpus centroid on rarity distribution, sentence statistics, and mechanical style markers. It catches em-dashes, Claude vocabulary, register mismatch, and rhythm uniformity.

**What the centrifuge is not:** a judge of quality. Every dimension is distance-to-a-fixed-centroid. It cannot distinguish "deviates because it's worse" from "deviates because it's new." A genuinely better essay can score lower than a mediocre one that hugs the mean. Chasing the score is Goodhart's law with extra steps; selection on the score would freeze the centroid and ossify the voice.

**Protocol:** Run it. Read the diagnosis. Investigate any flag by decomposing the sub-scores (which feature, what actual vs target). Then David's eye decides. New David-approved work re-measures the centroid — the box moves with the voice.

**Output:** Score + decomposed diagnosis. If MARGINAL or LOW, the decomposition tells you which phase to revisit — or tells you the scorer is measuring an intentional choice, which is also an answer.

## When to skip phases

**Short-form (LinkedIn reply, email):** Phases 1-3 collapse into a moment of thought. Phase 4 is the writing. Phase 5 might add one callback. Phases 6-7 are automatic.

**Medium-form (LinkedIn article, blog post):** Phase 1 is a quick conversation. Phase 2 is a quick search. Phase 3 is optional. Phases 4-7 are full.

**Long-form (essay, manifesto):** All seven phases, in order, no skipping.

**Major insertions into existing essays:** full pipeline for the new section — architecture, fresh ARM (with live verification when the material is recent), wireframe, draft, weapons, mechanical. A new section is a small essay with seams; "pipeline treatment," not inline patching.

---

## What this replaces

The old approach: dump voice_description.md + retrieved examples + register targets into a single system prompt, generate the full text, run autocorrect, score. That's Phase 4 alone. It misses the architecture, the ammunition, the surgical weapons pass, and the iterative quality check.

The old Phase 4 also carried a formula — "bimodal beat (short punches after long runs)" — which prescribed rhythm as a pattern. Killed 2026-07-20 and replaced by the Rhythm Doctrine: shape follows content, variance is emergent, formula is failure.

The new approach is slower. It should be. Writing that stabs takes longer than writing that informs.
