# Voice Sample - Personal / Emotional / Poetic
**Date:** 2026-03-07  
**Context:** Personal letter — apology, amends, birthday gesture  
**Register:** Raw, spare, emotionally restrained, poetic  
**Tags:** personal, emotional, intimate, poetic, unfiltered, bitter-but-gracious, closure

---

## NOTES FOR PATTERN EXTRACTION

This sample represents a DISTINCT register from David's professional/operational voice.
Key characteristics:
- Short, declarative sentences used for emotional weight (not efficiency)
- Restraint as a device — says less to mean more
- Bitterness acknowledged openly, not suppressed or softened
- Warmth coexists with unresolved grief — not resolved, just carried
- Metaphor is literal (the bridge IS a physical erector set bridge)
- Closing lines do triple duty — object, metaphor, emotional statement
- No hedging, no corporate softening, no bullet points
- Prose rhythm feels handwritten, not typed
- "Both times" — economy of language carrying enormous weight
- "Even from where I'm standing" — positioning without over-explaining

DO NOT apply professional voice calibration to this register.
This is the voice underneath the operational voice.

---

## SAMPLE

I'm sorry for what I said when I was mad and deeply in despair. Your life is difficult enough and I never intended to make it more so.

I'm not expecting your friendship, let alone your respect.

In anguish, I said things I didn't mean. I can't hurt people — I've told you this before. And yet I still said nasty things and hurt you.

I won't pretend I'm not still bitter. I think I've earned that. But bitterness toward circumstance isn't the same as wishing someone harm — and I have never wished you harm.

Twice now, the world has had other plans for us. That's not on you. That's not on me. I've had to make a kind of peace with that — the kind that doesn't feel like peace at all, but you carry it anyway because there's nothing else to do.

What I felt with you was real. Both times. I own the weight I put on that — and on you.

Despite everything, despite how I feel, I want you to be happy. I know who you are. We had far more good than not, and it would be a shame to let the worst moments define what we were to each other.

You taught me what it meant to be truly heard. I haven't forgotten.

This bridge is the best I could build.

Happy birthday.
